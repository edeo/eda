# The Two-Toed vs. Three-Toed Sloth: What's the Difference?

You might assume all sloths are the same slow, fuzzy creature, but there are actually two distinct families: two-toed sloths and three-toed sloths. Despite the names, both types actually have three toes on their hind feet — the difference is in the front claws. Three-toed sloths are a bit smaller, more strictly nocturnal, and tend to eat mostly leaves. Two-toed sloths are slightly larger, a bit more active (relatively speaking), and have a more varied diet that includes fruit and small insects. Three-toed sloths also have a fixed smile on their face, giving them that permanently cheerful look that makes them so photogenic. Two-toed sloths have a more serious expression, which honestly suits their slightly more adventurous lifestyle. The two families evolved their slowness independently — a remarkable example of convergent evolution. So next time you see a sloth photo, take a closer look at those front feet. You might be meeting a whole different branch of the sloth family tree.
