# Why Sloths Only Poop Once a Week

Of all the sloth's quirky habits, perhaps none is more remarkable than their bathroom schedule. Sloths descend from their trees approximately once a week to defecate on the ground — a risky journey that exposes them to predators. So why do it at all? Why not just go from the tree like most arboreal animals? Scientists have a few theories. One is that the sloth's dung drop is connected to the sloth moth: moths living in the sloth's fur lay eggs in the dung, and the resulting larvae contribute to algae growth on the sloth's coat, completing a tiny ecological loop. Another theory is that the weekly ritual may play a role in communication and reproduction. Whatever the reason, this weekly descent is genuinely dangerous — a significant portion of sloth deaths occur on the ground. It's a fascinating example of a behavior that seems inefficient on the surface but is likely part of a complex, finely tuned ecological relationship. The sloth's bathroom habits might just be one of the rainforest's most underappreciated dramas.
