# Sloths Are in Trouble: What's Threatening Them

Despite their laid-back reputation, sloths face a growing set of serious threats. Deforestation is the biggest: as tropical rainforests in Central and South America are cleared for agriculture and development, sloths lose the continuous canopy they depend on for movement and survival. Forced onto the ground or into fragmented forest patches, they become easy prey and struggle to find mates. Power lines running through former forest corridors are another major hazard — sloths mistake them for branches and suffer severe electrocution injuries. Road traffic kills many sloths each year as they attempt to cross cleared land between forest fragments. The illegal wildlife trade also takes a toll, as baby sloths are captured and sold as pets, often dying quickly without proper care. Climate change adds another layer of pressure, threatening the specific mix of tree species many sloths rely on for food. Organizations like the Sloth Conservation Foundation are working to address these threats through habitat protection, wildlife crossing infrastructure, and community education.
