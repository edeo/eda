# Baby Sloths: Six Months Clinging to Mom

Baby sloths enter the world already equipped with tiny claws and a strong grip — which is a good thing, since they'll spend the first six months of their lives clinging to their mother's fur. Born after about six months of gestation, sloth pups are surprisingly alert and capable from birth. They instinctively grab onto their mother and stay there, nursing and learning as she goes about her slow arboreal life. This extended contact period is critical. The baby learns which leaves and branches to use, what to eat, and how to navigate the canopy entirely by watching and riding along. After about six months, the mother begins to wean the pup and gradually expand its independence, though young sloths may remain in close proximity to their mother's home range for another year or more. Orphaned baby sloths require extraordinary care — without that early bonding experience, they often struggle to learn the specific survival skills their forest requires. Sloth sanctuaries in Costa Rica and Panama have made great strides in rehabilitation, but nothing quite replaces six months of hanging on for dear life.
