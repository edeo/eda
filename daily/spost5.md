# Sloths Are Surprisingly Good Swimmers

For an animal that barely moves on land, sloths are remarkably capable in the water. They can swim up to three times faster than they walk on the ground, using their long arms to pull through the water in a slow breaststroke. Sloths will sometimes drop directly from overhanging branches into rivers and lakes to cross to new territory or escape predators. Their large, buoyant stomachs — which can make up nearly a third of their body weight when full — help them float. They can also hold their breath for up to 40 minutes by slowing their heart rate, which is a useful trick for crossing slow-moving waterways in the Amazon basin. During seasonal flooding in tropical rainforests, swimming becomes an essential skill. Sloths have been spotted crossing wide rivers and even navigating ocean channels between islands off the coast of Central America. It's a reminder that animals rarely fit neatly into our expectations. The slowest mammal on land turns out to be a perfectly competent, if unhurried, swimmer.
